Golden Oyster Mushroom Research

Help Solve a Fungal Mystery!

Hi! I'm Aishwarya, a 3rd-year PhD student in the Pringle Lab at UW–Madison, and I study invasive Pleurotus citrinopileatus, also known as the golden oyster mushroom. This season (April–December 2025), I'm asking fellow mushroom lovers across North America to help by submitting wild golden oyster specimens for a population genomics study.

I'm especially looking for mushrooms collected outside Wisconsin—from places like Ontario, Texas, New York, Colorado, Alabama, and many more. Observations from states where golden oysters haven’t been recorded yet (like Rhode Island, Arkansas, or Maine) are especially valuable.

To contribute:

  • Find golden oyster mushrooms growing wild (not cultivated).
  • Take a photo (and optionally post to iNaturalist or MushroomObserver).
  • Collect 1–5 mushrooms in a brown or wax paper bag.
  • Dehydrate if you can (1–4 hrs), or refrigerate until shipping.
  • Include collection location, substrate info, and contact details.
  • Send to the address below (no plastic bags, please).

I’ll be culturing these mushrooms and analyzing their DNA to understand how cultivation and invasion may have shaped their evolution.
